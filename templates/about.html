About the data

This portal allows you to filter your research, by giving more weight to specific statistics and less importance to others.
The database considers:

  • The total number of students in the university
  • How many students are enrolled for one member of the teaching staff(Student/staff ratio)
  • The position obtained in the 2020 ranking list for all the universities as provided by the Times Higher Education's portal (THE rank position)
  • Percentage of international students
  • Percentage of Male/Female students
  • A score given by THE that reflects the quality of the teachings (THE Teaching score)
  • A score given by THE that reflects the quality and intensity of the researches (THE Research score)
  • A score given by THE that reflects the quality and frequency of the citations (THE Citation score)
  • A score given by THE that reflects the average industry income offered to the graduates (Industry Income)
  • A score given by THE that reflects the international outlook of the university (International outlook)
  • A score given by THE to further classify the universities based on the previous parameters. The value is obtained as 30% teaching, 30% research, 30% citations, 7.5% international outlook, 2.5% industry income (THE final score)
  • A new ranking position based on the THE final score computed (Rank by THE final score)


For the 24 universities belonging to the Russel Group, the portal provides a different database that focuses on the teachings and research's areas of the universities.
The fields chosen are:

  • Medicine. For all the teaching/researches focused on Medicine, Pharmaceutical and Veterinary
  • Life Science. For all the teaching/researches focused on Biology, Biotechnology, Neuroscience and Biochemistry
  • Social Science. For all the teaching/researches focused on Economics, Political sciences, Psychology, Sociology and Communication
  • Computer Science. For all the teaching/researches focused on ComputerScience, Data Science, Quantum world, Machine learning and inherent fields
  • Physical Science. For all the teaching/researches focused on Physics, Mathematics, Astronomy, Chemistry and Earth science
  • Engineering & Technology. For all the teaching/researches focused on engineering and inherent fields
  • Arts and Humanities. For all the teaching/researches focused on Education, History, Archaeology, Languages and Literature
  • Law. For all the teaching/researches focused on the Law sector
  • Business. For all the teaching/researches focused on the Business, Management and MBA
  • Theology. For all the teaching focused on Theology and Religious studies in general
  • Environmental studies. For all the researches focused on sustainable development and a greener future
